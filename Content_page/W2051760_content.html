Every Species Matters

Our planet faces an extinction crisis driven by human impacts like habitat loss and climate change. Iconic species are at risk. But we can protect threatened wildlife through conservation action. Measures like habitat protection and community engagement can prevent biodiversity loss. What we do now will define the future of life on Earth. By working together, we can safeguard endangered species.

Species in Danger

Amur Leopard

  • Around 84 individuals remaining
  • Found only in the border regions between Russia and China

Black Rhinoceros

  • Approximately 5,600 individuals remaining
  • Primarily found in South Africa, Namibia, Kenya, and Zimbabwe

Orangutan

  • Around 138,000 individuals remaining
  • Found in the rainforests of Borneo and Sumatra in Indonesia

Javan Rhino

  • Only around 72 individuals remaining
  • Found in Ujung Kulon National Park in Indonesia

Saola

  • fewer than 100 individuals remaining
  • Found in the Annamite Mountains along the border between Vietnam and Laos

Axolotl

  • Around 1,000 individuals remaining
  • Found in Xochimilco, a borough of Mexico City
